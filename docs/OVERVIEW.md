# **Storybook Introduction**

**Idea:**
The concept behind this CV is to present your journey as a full-stack developer like an epic story. Instead of a conventional list of experiences and skills, the website takes the visitor on a visual, narrative journey through your development career—like flipping through a storybook. Each “chapter” represents a phase of your professional life, combining illustrations, interactive elements, and storytelling to make your skills, projects, and experiences memorable. The aim is to convey not just what you can do, but **how you grow, solve problems, and take ownership of projects**—all in an engaging, visually appealing format.

**Introduction Text (Story Style):**

> “Once upon a time in the city of Taroudant, a curious developer named Yassine began a journey into the world of code. With nothing but curiosity and determination, he explored the realms of backend and frontend, learning to connect the invisible threads that make digital worlds come alive.
>
> This story is not just about lines of code—it’s about quests undertaken, challenges conquered, and tools mastered. Flip through these pages to follow the adventures of a full-stack developer, from the first self-taught experiments to complex projects shaping the way people interact with technology today. Welcome to my story.”

# **Storybook CV: Visual & UX Blueprint**

## **General Site Features**

* **Layout:** Full-page “storybook” sections, each chapter = one scrollable page.
* **Navigation:**

  * Arrow buttons or scroll snap between chapters.
  * Optional “table of contents” sidebar for jumping chapters.
* **Animations:**

  * Smooth fade-in/out for text and images.
  * Parallax background layers for depth.
  * Hover animations on skills/tools (bounce, glow, or rotate).
  * GIFs or small looping animations for interactions (e.g., a code snippet typing animation).
* **Responsive:** Works on desktop and mobile (maybe slightly simplified for mobile).
